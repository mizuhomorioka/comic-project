Ever since I came to New York, I have seen many people wearing T shirts with Japanese comic character. Probably Japanese comics are more popluar than I thougt.

But how popular and powerful are they?

I scraped a Wikipedia page named "List of best-selling comic series, where only comics that have sold more than 100 million copies are collected.

In the Wikipedia, there are two lists, the list of comics printed in a traditional book format and the list of floppy comics known as the American comic book format, so I combined them to see the trends.

Evnetually, there are 37 comic series in the list of million-selling comic series.

This is the top 10 comic series among the 37 "million-selling" comic series.

Superman is number one, with 600 million copies. One piece is in the second, with 516 million.

Breaking the top 10 series by country, U.S. has the most series with 4, followed by Japan in second place with three. China, Belgium and France each has one series.

In the list, Which coutry has produced million-selling copies the most?

Sorted by country, Japan produced the most, 19 series in the list. The U.S. is in the second, with 10 series. Belgium produced 3 series, such as "The Adventure of Tintin." Italy, France, China prduced each one series.

Most of the best-selling comic series are created in Japan and U.S.

Let's see the recent trend of million-selling comics.

This chart indicates that when million-selling comic series started to be serialized, and which country they belong to.

Surprisingly, among 15 series serialized after 1980, 14 series were produced in Japan.

Most recent comic series is "Damon Slayer:Kimetsu no Yaiba" and it was serialized from 2016 to 2020. Dispite this short term, it has sold at least 150 million copies.
